Working with matrix

Ways to create matrix:

> matrix(nrow=2,ncol=2)

     [,1] [,2]
[1,]   NA   NA
[2,]   NA   NA

> matrix(1:6,nrow=2,ncol=3)

     [,1] [,2] [,3]
[1,]    1    3    5
[2,]    2    4    6

>Z<-trix(6:1,now=2,ncol=3)

> Z 

     [,1] [,2] [,3]
[1,]    6    4    2
[2,]    5    3    1

> A<-matrix(data=c(8,6,5,5,4,6,9,1,3),nrow=3)

> A

     [,1] [,2] [,3]
[1,]    8    5    9
[2,]    6    4    1
[3,]    5    6    3

compute a determinant of a matrix

> det(A)

[1] 127

Transpose of a matrix

> t(Z) 

     [,1] [,2]
[1,]    6    5
[2,]    4    3
[3,]    2    1

Inverse of a matrix

> solve(A)

            [,1]       [,2]        [,3]
[1,]  0.04724409  0.3070866 -0.24409449
[2,] -0.10236220 -0.1653543  0.36220472
[3,]  0.12598425 -0.1811024  0.01574803
